Health in Perspective

Health in Perspective is a health policy journalism portfolio (hosted on the Beehiiv platform) that explores the intersection between public health and government policy. By detailing how health policy affects stakeholders and communities, I hope to investigate different means of improving access to and quality of care.

Journal Publications

Health in Perspective articles cover a wide variety of health policy related issues and case studies. Below are a few topics covered by my previous articles:

  • Who Controls Health? A Complex Care System Network: Health Economics of Dialysis Treatment in India.
  • Case Study. Policy Determines Health Outcomes: The Flint Michigan Water Crisis.
  • Bridging the Gap. The Oregon Health Plan: A Healthcare Access Experiment.
  • Quality vs Quantity? Maximizing Care: Rural Hospital Closures and Physician Shortage.

Themes:

I am always looking for new ways to expand my audience! Over the past several months, I have focused my articles around 10 specific themes (argumentation and framing, environmental conservation, North Carolina-specific issues, Government Programs, Social Determinants of Health, Healthcare Access, International Policy, Public Health Case Studies, Education-Related News, and Infectious Disease).

In the future, I hope to expand these articles as I learn more about health policy and become more involved in the field of public health.
